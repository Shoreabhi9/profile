About Me

Hello! I'm Abhinav Mishra, a passionate physicist, avid runner, and lifelong learner. With an MSc in Physics, I've dedicated my life to understanding the fundamental workings of the universe while also pursuing my other passions.

My journey in physics began when I first read about Homi J. Bhabha's work as a teenager. Since then, I've been fascinated by quantum mechanics, astrophysics, and the intersection of theoretical and applied physics. When I'm not buried in equations, you'll find me running trails (slowly but steadily), collecting books I swear I'll read someday, or trying to explain complex physics concepts to anyone who'll listen (and many who won't).

I believe science should be accessible to everyone, which is why I enjoy breaking down complex ideas into understandable bits. My running habit keeps me sane (mostly) and provides the perfect time to think about physics problems. I'm particularly interested in quantum field theory and how it might help us understand the universe at its most fundamental level.

My Scientific Heroes

These brilliant minds have shaped my understanding of physics and inspired my journey:

Homi J. Bhabha

1909-1966

The father of India's nuclear program, Bhabha was a visionary physicist who established the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research and the Atomic Energy Establishment. His work on cosmic rays and quantum theory laid the foundation for India's scientific progress.

Key Contribution: Bhabha scattering in quantum electrodynamics

Albert Einstein

1879-1955

The most famous physicist of all time revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and gravity with his theories of relativity. His work on the photoelectric effect earned him the Nobel Prize and helped establish quantum theory.

Key Contribution: Theory of General Relativity

APJ Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Known as the Missile Man of India, Kalam was a brilliant aerospace engineer who played key roles in India's space and missile programs. His leadership at ISRO and DRDO helped establish India as a space-faring nation.

Key Contribution: Development of SLV-III and ballistic missile technology
